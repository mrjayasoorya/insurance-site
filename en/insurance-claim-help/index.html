Insurance claim help (what to do first)

Claim delays are usually caused by late reporting, missing proof, unclear timelines, or policy wording mismatches. This page explains a practical claim workflow—without promising outcomes.

A practical claim workflow

  1. Safety first. Then capture photos/videos (as applicable) before repairs.
  2. Inform insurer/agent early (don’t wait until next day).
  3. Keep a clean incident timeline (what happened first/second/third).
  4. Collect documents: policy copy/endorsements, IDs, reports if applicable.
  5. Follow survey/inspection steps if required before repairs.

What commonly delays claims

  • Late intimation/reporting.
  • No clean incident timeline or missing first-day photos (as applicable).
  • Missing invoices/valuation proof for stock/assets (as applicable).
  • Mismatch between operations and policy wording.

FAQs

Questions people ask ›
Do I always need an FIR? Not always. FIR requirements depend on incident type, third-party involvement, injury/theft and insurer process. We guide the correct path for your case.
Can I start repairs immediately? Avoid repairs before photos and insurer guidance. Early repairs can remove proof; follow survey/inspection steps if required.
Do you guarantee claim approval? No. We help you follow correct steps and keep evidence/timelines clean to reduce avoidable delays.
What are the most important documents? Policy copy + endorsements, incident timeline, first-day photos/videos (as applicable), and required reports when applicable.
